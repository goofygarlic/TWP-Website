Expanded Preface

In this expanded preface, I delve deeper into the motivations and experiences behind the TWP project. The idea initially came from a passing conversation about how our possessions shape our identity and how discarding items can often go unnoticed. I wanted to shine a light on the everyday objects we interact with. (TEMPLATE)

Through recording my trash, I became more aware of trends in my buying habits: impulse purchases, recurring staples, and the guilt of throwing out items before fully using them. This small act of documentation helped me see the broader implications of consumer culture and waste management. Over time, I refined my approach to incorporate minimalism and learned how to distinguish between fleeting wants and genuine needs. (TEMPLATE)

The Trash of the Week series also led me to question the sustainability aspects behind each product I bought. Packaging materials, shipping distances, and manufacturing processes all factored into the real cost of any item I ultimately threw away. This reflection has encouraged me to buy second-hand or eco-friendly alternatives when possible and to share these considerations with family and friends. (TEMPLATE)

Ultimately, this project is part introspective journal, part environmental activism. By paying attention to the items that come and go in my life, I'm taking small but tangible steps toward reducing waste and building awareness. I hope that by sharing this journey, others might take an interest in their own daily discards and, in turn, become more conscious consumers. (TEMPLATE)
